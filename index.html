IOS

Our systems currently rely heavily on external software ecosystems and foreign-managed infrastructure to operate essential services.
This dependence introduces significant strategic vulnerabilities, including risks of data interception, unauthorized access, supply chain compromise, and targeted digital disruption during periods of geopolitical tension or conflict.

As global cyber threats continue to evolve in complexity and scale, it has become increasingly important to strengthen our digital sovereignty and reduce exposure to external points of failure.
This includes ensuring that critical national systems can operate independently, even in the event of global network instability or restricted access to foreign platforms.

To address these challenges, we are introducing a new independent Operating System designed with a strong focus on stability, security, and long-term resilience.
It is built to support essential infrastructure, reduce reliance on external vendors, and provide a controlled and transparent environment for system operations.

This operating system incorporates modern security architecture, hardened access controls, and optimized performance layers intended for mission-critical use cases across public and private sectors.
It is also designed to be scalable, allowing future expansion while maintaining strict security and governance standards.

We remain committed to building a safer and more self-reliant digital ecosystem that empowers our institutions and protects our national data assets.

We hope our community finds this new system reliable, efficient, and worthy of trust as we move toward a more secure and connected future.
